Dengue

Dengue (break-bone fever) is a viral infection that spreads from mosquitoes to people. It is more common in tropical and subtropical climates.


What are the causes of Dengue?

Dengue is a viral infection transmitted by the bite of the female Aedes mosquito which breeds in free-standing water.


What are the symptoms of Dengue?

Most people with dengue have mild or no symptoms and will get better in 1–2 weeks. Rarely, dengue can be severe and lead to death.
If symptoms occur, they usually begin 4–10 days after infection and last for 2–7 days. Symptoms may include:

  • high fever (40°C/104°F).
  • severe headache.
  • pain behind the eyes.
  • muscle and joint pains.
  • nausea.
  • vomiting.
  • swollen glands.
  • rash.


How do you treat Dengue?

Most cases of dengue fever can be treated at home with pain medicine. Preventing mosquito bites is the best way to avoid getting dengue. There is no specific treatment for dengue. The focus is on treating pain symptoms. Acetaminophen (paracetamol) is often used to control pain. Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs like ibuprofen and aspirin are avoided as they can increase the risk of bleeding.

How to Prevent Dengue
Here are some things you can do to avoid the Dengue.
  1. Use insect repellent.
  2. Take steps to control mosquitoes indoors and outdoors.
  3. Use screens on windows and doors. Repair holes in screens to keep mosquitoes outdoors.
  4. Stop mosquitoes from laying eggs in or near water.by keeping surroundings clean.
